Projects

Gameplay & Automation

This project was designed to test the subjective experience of users engaged with automated and manual processes. Auto-prompt features have been built in to make sure users pay attention to the screen when the dino-bot is completing the task for them. This project will be published in the fall to collect data and I will analyze and build user portfolio based on their demographic and personality characteristics.

Development Tools: Python, JavaScript, HTML, & Firebase

Chatbot & Wellness

This chatbot was designed with the goal of assessing users' individualized wellness definitions and domain satisfaction. With a multi-method design (qualitative + quantitative), I conducted A/B Testing to assess user preferences between a basic survey platform (Qualtrics) and Chatbot delivery. I also performed usability tests and content analysis to analyze users' overall experience with our platforms.

Development and Analysis Tools:
Python, R, JavaScript, HTML, SpeechAI, & Firebase

Web-based Mental Health Training

This training website was originally developed on Qualtrics, then was upgraded with the use of Python, JavaScript, jsPsych, and Firebase to collect data on studentsâ€™ wellbeing and coping abilities for two years (Sample Code). Training materials have been continually improved based on evaluations from iterative usability tests.

This study was published in the Journal of Anxiety, Stress, & Coping, and presented at the Society for Personality and Social Psychology (SPSP) 2022 Conference and the American Psychological Association (APA) 2021 Conference.

Self-Control & Decision Making

This project studied the impact of short-term versus long-term financial decision making patterns for people of varying incomes and ethnic backgrounds using an online survey I made in Qualtrics.

This study was presented at the CalABA 2020 Conference

User Behavior Assessment

This online study was created with JavaScript, jsPsych, and Firebase to collect data on video game players. Qualitative and quantitative data analysese were conducted to understand antecedents and consequences of user behaviours in Python.

This study was presented at the Association for Behavior Analysis (ABAI) 2020 Conference

Multilingual Mindfulness Training & Physical Activity

This project helped international students improve exercise behaviours with the use of mindfulness training. Quantitative (e.g., steps, gym visits) and qualitative data (e.g., individual preferences, goal setting) were collected to write and analyze concurrent progress reports in R.

This study was orally presented and published in the Journal of Contextual Behavioral Science.
